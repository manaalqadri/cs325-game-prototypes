Split Maze

Originality

  • I am not sure of the originality of this game. I was most likely influeneced by puzzle games I used to play. A completed game would have more shapes, similar to those pipe games or train track building games. The board would allow for a snapping feature so that it's easier to place the pieces or buttons that rotate through the shapes. There would be a feature to make sure that once a piece is confirmed, it wouldn't be able to move again.

    • Prototype Goal:

  • This prototype laid the foundation, but there was a lot more that I wanted to do. I wanted to be able to create collisions so that the ball wouldn't be able to go over the borders place. I also wanted the borders to be placed once and not move again. Right now it plays like a digital analog game.

    • Player Experience Goals:

  • I want the players to feel a sort of competitveness as they go through the game.

    • Gameplay:

  • There are two types of players that are categorized by color. Blue: This player controls the ball. Their goal is to get to the goal that is blue while trying to avoid getting pushed to the orange goal. Orange: This player controls the borders. Their goal is to guide the player to their orange goal. There are a limited number of borders so they have to use their pieces wisely Each player can put/move at most, 2 pieces/spaces. The ball can more in any direction, up, down, left, right, and diagonally.

    • Strategies:

  • Blue players can move endlessly until the orange players run out of pieces. At that point, it's a straight path to their own goal
