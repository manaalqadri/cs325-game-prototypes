Pirate Island

Originality

  • This uses the "First Game" example. I used the base code to get the coins to disable when overlapped with the ship, After that, the concept in making the ship jump with the water was my own. I played around with the physics and overlaying images so that the wave was hidden under the ship. Unfortunately, the wave can collect the coins along with the ship. That was not what I was tring to do but became a byproduct of the implementation.

    • Technical Merit

  • Camera follows the player, there's a parallax on the background, the coins float and are auto generated, and the player can only move in one direction

    • Prototype Postmortem:

  • I learned how to get the camera to follow the sprite as well as make prallax backgrounds so the game looked like it had more depth. The parallax was the easiest part of the game. It was one function connected to the images. The hardest was getting the coins to disappear and collect the score

    • Art is all unique (made by myself)

    • High Concept:

  • Help the pirates collect coins and get back home to Skullocto Island

    Theme:

  • Pirate

    Mandated Variety:

  • Side-Scroller, Deterministic

    Prototype Goal:

  • Camera movement. The camera should follow the ship as it moves through the game.

    Player Experience Goals:

  • I'd hope that collecting is something they would like to do. In the future, I would want to make different objects that players can collect.

    Gameplay:

  • The player can only go towards the right and jump. They can't go left (back) to pick up coins they left behind. The player can then play the game to see how best to collect all the coins.

    • Strategies:

  • Best thing to do is go slow.

    • Story/Setting/Premise:

  • The pirates have been been pillaging and collecting treasure. Help them get back home to Skullocto Island and collect coins along the way.
